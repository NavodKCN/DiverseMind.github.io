Project Scope

Literature Survey

Recent research has developed specialized tools for students with learning challenges, though most focus on diagnosed disabilities rather than slow learners, particularly in Sri Lanka. For instance, platforms like “Writaupair” [1] use NLP to assess writing issues but are not suited for brief writing tasks typical in primary education. Attention span tools [2] assess engagement using facial and behavioral cues, achieving 84.62% accuracy, yet they lack emotional interpretation and real-time intervention. In mathematics, gamified tools such as “NUMERATica” [3] enhance learning through gradual challenges, proving effective for slow learners. Meanwhile, systems like PLAS [4] address working memory by analyzing student data to offer support, though they depend heavily on historical performance and lack cognitive assessments. Despite these innovations, most solutions isolate identification from intervention and often overlook the local educational context of Sri Lankan learners."DiverseMind" aims to bridge these gaps by providing comprehensive screening and intervention for slow learners across multiple cognitive domains.

Research Gap

High prevalence of learning difficulties

Nearly 30% of children in Sri Lanka face learning difficulties, creating an urgent need for targeted interventions. Current approaches fail to address the unique challenges of slow learners without formal diagnoses.

Educational dropout rates

Despite Sri Lanka's high primary education enrollment rate (99.11% in 2018), there is a significant drop at the secondary level (91.04%). Slow learners are particularly vulnerable to dropping out due to persistent academic struggles.

Lack of integrated support systems

Current support systems in Sri Lanka lack comprehensiveness, failing to identify slow learners early and provide targeted interventions. Most existing tools address either identification or intervention separately.

Identification and support for slow learners

Slow learners, defined as children with IQ between 70–85, are inadequately supported within traditional educational settings. Current tools often use outdated methodologies instead of incorporating modern technologies.

Research Problem

How to identify and support slow learners in Sri Lankan primary schools through integrated digital solutions?

Nearly 30% of Sri Lankan children face learning difficulties, with slow learners (IQ 70-85) being particularly vulnerable yet often unidentified within traditional educational systems. Despite high primary enrollment rates (99.11%), secondary education shows concerning dropout rates (91.04%), especially affecting children with learning challenges. Current approaches lack early detection mechanisms and integrated support systems tailored to Sri Lankan context. There is an urgent need for a comprehensive web-based platform that can effectively evaluate writing skills, attention span, mathematical abilities, and working memory while providing real-time interventions. This research aims to develop a contextually relevant digital solution that incorporates modern technologies to improve educational outcomes for slow learners in resource-limited settings.

Main Objective

This research aims to develop a web-based platform to identify and support slow learners among primary school children in Sri Lanka by leveraging advanced Machine Learning, image processing, and a multi-modal system architecture to assess key learning challenges including attention span, writing abilities, mathematical skills, and short-term memory. The platform will provide personalized, age-appropriate interventions through gamified methods designed to enhance student engagement and motivation, with the ultimate goal of fostering an inclusive educational environment that enables early identification of learning difficulties and improves academic performance and confidence in slow learners through interactive and effective support.

Specific Objectives

Writing Skills: Identification & Enhancement

To identify Grade 4 slow learners in Sri Lanka who experience challenges in writing skills such as letter formation and punctuation and provide early Sinhala language interventions through engaging activities that improve writing ability, boost confidence, and enhance academic performance.

Attention Span: Identification & Improvement

To identify primary school children in Sri Lanka with short attention spans using face detection, gaze estimation, and head posture analysis, and to provide engaging Sinhala activities that improve attention, strengthen cognitive skills, and enhance overall learning outcomes.

Math Skills: Identification & Development

To identify Grade 4 students in Sri Lanka struggling with mathematics using machine learning, and to provide Sinhala-language, syllabus-aligned interactive exercises that strengthen math skills, boost confidence, and promote equal learning opportunities within the classroom environment.

Working Memory: Assessment & Strengthening

To identify primary school children with working memory challenges using machine learning and cognitive assessments, and to provide tailored web-based interventions such as gamified activities and multisensory strategies that strengthen working memory and support improved learning outcomes.

Our Solution

DiverseMind provides a comprehensive web-based platform to identify and support slow learners in Sri Lankan primary schools. The system evaluates four critical cognitive domains through advanced technologies. Writing skills assessment utilizes Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) with 98% accuracy. Mathematical abilities evaluation employs Decision Tree (DT) classifiers achieving 98% accuracy. Attention span measurement uses facial landmark tracking,gaze tracking and emotion recognition with 79.69% accuracy. Working memory assessment implements ML-driven cognitive tasks demonstrating 99% accuracy. The platform delivers real-time feedback and personalized interventions tailored to the Sri Lankan curriculum and Sinhala language. Teachers monitor student progress through a dedicated dashboard. DiverseMind incorporates gamification elements and interactive exercises to create an engaging learning environment for students. The system bridges the identification-intervention gap by providing both diagnostic tools and targeted support, helping slow learners overcome specific challenges within resource-limited educational settings. This integrated approach ensures early detection and timely intervention, potentially reducing dropout rates and improving long-term academic outcomes.
